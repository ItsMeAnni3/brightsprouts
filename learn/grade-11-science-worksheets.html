What your child will learn — Physics: Energy & Electricity

  • Energy forms: kinetic (motion), potential (stored), thermal, chemical, electrical, nuclear. A rollercoaster at the top is pure potential; the drop converts it to kinetic!
  • Conservation of energy: total energy stays constant. KE = ½mv², PE = mgh.
  • Electricity is flowing electrons. Ohm's Law: V = IR (voltage = current × resistance). Circuits need a closed loop.
  • Series circuits share one path (one bulb dies, all die); parallel circuits give each device its own path — that's how your house is wired.

Sample worksheet questions

1. Calculate KE of a 4 kg object at 3 m/s.
Answer: KE = ½ × 4 × 9 = 18 joules
2. Why are homes wired in parallel, not series?
Answer: Each device gets its own path — one failing doesn't cut power to everything
3. A rollercoaster at the peak has mostly what energy? At the bottom?
Answer: Potential at the peak; kinetic at the bottom
4. Using V = IR: what voltage drives 2 amps through 6 ohms?
Answer: 12 volts
5. In a series circuit, what happens when one bulb burns out?
Answer: All the bulbs go out — there's only one path
