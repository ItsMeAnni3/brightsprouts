What your child will learn — Digraphs, Blends & Silent e

  • A digraph is two letters making ONE sound: sh (ship), ch (chip), th (thin), wh (when).
  • A blend is two letters you can still hear separately: bl (black), st (stop), fr (frog), sk (skip).
  • Silent e sits at the end of a word, says nothing, and makes the vowel say its own name: hop → hope, kit → kite.
  • That is why 'cap' and 'cape' look so similar but sound so different.
  • Keep collecting sight words — they appear in almost every sentence you read.

Sample worksheet questions

1. Which two letters make the sound at the start of 'thin'?
Answer: t and h (th)
2. Add a silent e to 'hop'. What word do you get?
Answer: hope
3. Is 'sh' a digraph or a blend?
Answer: A digraph
4. Do you say the 'e' in 'cape' out loud?
Answer: No — it is silent
5. What does a silent e do to the vowel before it?
Answer: Makes it say its own name (a long vowel)
