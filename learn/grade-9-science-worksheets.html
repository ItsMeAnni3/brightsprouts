What your child will learn — Biology: DNA & Genetics

  • DNA is a double helix — a twisted ladder whose rungs are base pairs: A pairs with T, C pairs with G.
  • A gene is a section of DNA that codes for a trait. Humans have about 20,000 genes on 46 chromosomes (23 from each parent).
  • Dominant alleles (B) mask recessive ones (b). Two brown-eye-gene parents can have a blue-eyed child if both carry a hidden 'b'.
  • Punnett squares predict trait probability: cross Bb × Bb and there's a 1-in-4 chance of bb (recessive trait shows).

Sample worksheet questions

1. How many chromosomes do humans have, and from where?
Answer: 46 — 23 from each parent
2. What is a gene?
Answer: A section of DNA that codes for a trait
3. In a Bb × Bb cross, what fraction of offspring show the recessive trait?
Answer: 1/4 (the bb combination)
4. In genetics, what does a capital letter like 'B' represent?
Answer: A dominant allele
5. Why can two brown-eyed parents have a blue-eyed child?
Answer: Both can carry a hidden recessive allele and each pass it on
