Key ideas

  • Scientists measure Earth's story in millions and even BILLIONS of years. A billion is a thousand millions — a HUGE amount of time!
  • We learn about deep time from clues: rocks, the layers in the ground, and fossils (the preserved bones and prints of ancient living things).
  • These dates are science's best estimates from lots and lots of evidence. Earth itself is about 4.5 billion years old.
  • This whole journey happened BEFORE the Stone Age. Humans arrive only at the very, very end — you're about to see just how new we really are!

Sample worksheet questions

1. What caused the dinosaurs to die out about 66 million years ago?
Answer: A giant asteroid hit the Earth
2. Put these in order, oldest first: dinosaurs, the Big Bang, the first human ancestors.
Answer: The Big Bang, then the dinosaurs, then the first human ancestors
3. A billion is how many millions?
Answer: A thousand millions (1,000)
4. About how long ago did the Big Bang happen?
Answer: About 13.8 billion years ago
5. What tiny living things slowly filled Earth's air with oxygen?
Answer: Cyanobacteria (tiny microbes) using sunlight
