What your child will learn — Chemistry: Atoms & the Periodic Table

  • Atoms have a nucleus of protons (+) and neutrons (0), orbited by electrons (−). The proton count (atomic number) defines the element.
  • The periodic table is organized by atomic number; columns (groups) share behavior — group 18, the noble gases, barely react at all.
  • Chemical bonds: ionic (electrons transferred — Na⁺Cl⁻, table salt) and covalent (electrons shared — H₂O).
  • Chemical reactions rearrange atoms but never destroy them: mass is conserved. Equations must balance!

Sample worksheet questions

1. What three particles make up an atom, and their charges?
Answer: Protons (+), neutrons (neutral), electrons (−)
2. What number defines which element an atom is?
Answer: The atomic number — its count of protons
3. In H₂O, what type of bond holds the atoms together?
Answer: Covalent (electrons are shared)
4. Which group of elements barely reacts at all?
Answer: Group 18 — the noble gases
5. What does 'conservation of mass' mean in a reaction?
Answer: Atoms are rearranged, not created or destroyed — total mass stays equal
