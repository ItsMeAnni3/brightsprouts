Every town has rules and leaders. Local government is the part of government closest to your own front door.

What your child will learn — My Community & Its Government

  • A local government runs a town or city, led by a mayor and a city council.
  • Citizens vote to choose their leaders — that is called an election.
  • Laws are rules made by a government, and everyone must follow them.
  • Local government provides services: schools, police, firefighters, libraries, parks and rubbish collection.
  • The three levels of government in the US are local, state and national (federal).

Sample worksheet questions

1. What is a law?
Answer: A rule made by a government
2. Name two services local government provides.
Answer: e.g. schools, police, libraries, parks
3. Who leads a city government?
Answer: A mayor
4. Why do communities need laws?
Answer: To keep people safe and treat everyone fairly
5. What is a citizen?
Answer: A member of a country with rights and responsibilities
