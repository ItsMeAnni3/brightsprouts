What your child will learn — Algebra II: Functions, Exponents & Trig

  • Exponent laws: x³·x⁴ = x⁷ (add), (x³)⁴ = x¹² (multiply), x⁻² = 1/x². Anything⁰ = 1.
  • Exponential growth: y = a·bˣ. Money at 5% interest: balance = 1000·(1.05)ᵗ — it curves upward, unlike a straight line!
  • Function notation: f(x) = 2x + 1 means 'plug in x'. f(3) = 7. Composition g(f(x)) chains machines together.
  • SOH-CAH-TOA: sin = opposite/hypotenuse, cos = adjacent/hypotenuse, tan = opposite/adjacent. With one angle and one side, you can find a flagpole's height from its shadow!

Sample worksheet questions

1. Simplify: x⁵ · x⁴ = ____
Answer: x⁹
2. If f(x) = 4x² − 5x, find f(4). ____
Answer: 44
3. A colony of 500 bacteria doubles every hour. How many after 2 hours? ____
Answer: 2000
4. In a right triangle with legs 3 and 4 (hypotenuse 5), find sin of the angle opposite the side of length 3. ____
Answer: 3/5
5. Simplify: x⁵ · x⁶ = ____
Answer: x¹¹
