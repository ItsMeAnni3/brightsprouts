What your child will learn — Point of View & Perspective

  • First person: the narrator IS a character ('I raced down the hall'). You see only what they see.
  • Third person limited: an outside narrator follows one character's thoughts.
  • Third person omniscient: the narrator knows EVERYONE'S thoughts — like a mind-reading drone.
  • Ask: Can I trust this narrator? What might they be missing, hiding, or exaggerating?

Sample worksheet questions

1. What is the narrator's final insight?
Answer: A whole community can misjudge someone
2. How does the narrator's opinion of the old man change?
Answer: From believing he was mean to realizing he is quietly kind
3. What point of view is this story told in? How do you know?
Answer: First person — the narrator uses 'I' and 'my'
4. What broke on the narrator's bike?
Answer: The bike chain
5. How would this story differ if the old man narrated it?
Answer: We'd know his thoughts/feelings — perhaps loneliness or amusement — instead of the kid's fear
