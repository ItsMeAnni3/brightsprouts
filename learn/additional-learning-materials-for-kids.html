Key ideas

  • Each square shows the ATOMIC NUMBER (how many protons the atom has), the SYMBOL, the NAME, and the ATOMIC MASS (how heavy one atom is).
  • The atomic number is the element's fingerprint: 1 proton is always hydrogen, 6 protons is always carbon — change the protons and you change the element!
  • Rows are called PERIODS (1–7). Columns are called GROUPS (1–18). Elements in the same group behave alike, because they have the same number of outer electrons.
  • Colour families: metals sit on the left, non-metals on the right, and the noble gases (far right column) are the loners — they hardly react with anything.
  • The two rows floating below are the lanthanides and actinides. They belong in the gaps of rows 6 and 7, but the table would be too wide to print, so we pull them out!

Sample worksheet questions

1. What is the chemical symbol for Nickel? ______
Answer: Ni
2. Which element has the symbol Sb? ______
Answer: Antimony
3. What is the atomic number of Silicon (Si)? ______
Answer: 14
4. Rutherfordium (Rf) has 104 protons. How many protons does an atom of Rutherfordium have? ______
Answer: 104
5. What is the chemical symbol for Flerovium? ______
Answer: Fl
