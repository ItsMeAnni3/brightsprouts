What your child will learn — Author's Purpose & Tone

  • The big three purposes: to Persuade, to Inform, to Entertain (remember 'PIE').
  • Tone is the author's attitude: sarcastic, urgent, playful, formal. It's the 'voice' you hear in your head.
  • Word choice reveals tone: 'the crowd trudged' vs 'the crowd bounced' paint different moods.
  • Ads, reviews, and speeches often mix purposes — an ad may entertain in order to persuade!

Sample worksheet questions

1. Why might humor persuade teens better than a stern lecture?
Answer: It keeps attention and avoids sounding preachy
2. Which phrases create the humorous tone?
Answer: Examples: 'not a food group', 'neon cheese puffs', 'highly scientific institution', 'be a hero'
3. What percentage statistic does the speech jokingly cite?
Answer: 34% fewer complaints about pop quizzes
4. What is the author's main purpose?
Answer: To persuade students to eat healthier (with entertainment mixed in)
5. How would you describe the tone?
Answer: Humorous, playful, mock-serious
