What your child will learn — Forces, Motion & Newton's Laws

  • Newton's 1st Law (Inertia): objects keep doing what they're doing unless a force acts. Seatbelts exist because YOUR body keeps moving when the car stops!
  • Newton's 2nd Law: Force = mass × acceleration. Heavier objects need more force to speed up — pushing a bike vs. pushing a truck.
  • Newton's 3rd Law: every action has an equal and opposite reaction. Rockets push gas DOWN, gas pushes rocket UP.
  • Friction is a force that resists sliding; gravity pulls objects toward each other — Earth pulls you at 9.8 m/s²!

Sample worksheet questions

1. Why do seatbelts save lives, in physics terms?
Answer: Inertia — your body keeps moving at car speed until a force (the belt, not the windshield) stops it
2. How does the 3rd law explain a rocket launch?
Answer: The rocket pushes exhaust down; the exhaust pushes the rocket up equally
3. Which law explains why you lurch forward when a car brakes?
Answer: Newton's 1st Law — inertia
4. Using F = ma, how much force accelerates a 10 kg box at 3 m/s²?
Answer: 30 newtons (10 × 3)
5. What force pulls you toward Earth at 9.8 m/s²?
Answer: Gravity
