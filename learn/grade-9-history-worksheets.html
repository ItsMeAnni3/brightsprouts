What your child will learn — Revolutions & the Industrial Age

  • The Enlightenment argued for reason, natural rights and government by consent — ideas that fuelled revolutions.
  • The American and French Revolutions both challenged rule by kings; the French Revolution began in 1789.
  • The Industrial Revolution began in Britain with textiles, steam power and coal.
  • Factory work brought cities, wages and terrible conditions, including child labour, prompting unions and reform laws.
  • Industrial powers then competed for colonies and raw materials, driving a new wave of imperialism across Africa and Asia.

Sample worksheet questions

1. In what year did the French Revolution begin?
Answer: 1789
2. Why did industrial nations want colonies?
Answer: For raw materials and markets
3. What did workers form to demand better conditions?
Answer: Unions
4. What is imperialism?
Answer: Powerful nations taking control of other territories
5. Name a problem of early factory work.
Answer: Child labour, long hours or dangerous conditions
