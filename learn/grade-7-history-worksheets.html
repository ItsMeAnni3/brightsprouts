What your child will learn — The Middle Ages

  • Feudalism was a system of land in exchange for loyalty and service: kings granted land to nobles, who owed them soldiers.
  • Most people were peasants or serfs who farmed a lord's land and kept little of what they grew.
  • The Catholic Church was the most powerful institution in medieval Europe, and monasteries preserved learning.
  • Islamic civilization flourished at the same time, advancing mathematics, medicine and astronomy and preserving Greek texts.
  • The Black Death, a plague arriving in Europe in the 1340s, killed roughly a third of the population and weakened feudalism.

Sample worksheet questions

1. What was the most powerful institution in medieval Europe?
Answer: The Catholic Church
2. Who farmed the land and kept little of it?
Answer: Peasants and serfs
3. Who sat at the top of the feudal pyramid?
Answer: The king
4. About how much of Europe's population died?
Answer: About one third
5. What was the Black Death?
Answer: A plague that struck Europe in the 1340s
