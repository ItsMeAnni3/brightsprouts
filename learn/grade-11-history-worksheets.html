What your child will learn — The Gilded Age & Progressive Era

  • After the Civil War, industry, railroads and steel transformed the US economy.
  • Millions of immigrants arrived, many through Ellis Island, supplying labour and reshaping American cities.
  • Enormous fortunes sat beside tenement poverty, child labour and unsafe factories.
  • Progressive reformers pushed for safety laws, limits on child labour, food and drug inspection and trust-busting.
  • The women's suffrage movement won the vote with the 19th Amendment in 1920.

Sample worksheet questions

1. Name a problem of the Gilded Age.
Answer: Child labour, unsafe factories or tenement poverty
2. In what year was it ratified?
Answer: 1920
3. What did Progressive reformers want?
Answer: Safety laws, limits on child labour, inspection and trust-busting
4. What is a trust, in this era?
Answer: A giant business combination that limited competition
5. Where did many immigrants arrive in New York?
Answer: Ellis Island
