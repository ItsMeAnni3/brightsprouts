What your child will learn — The Five Regions of the United States

  • The five regions are the Northeast, Southeast, Midwest, Southwest and West.
  • The Northeast has big cities and the earliest colonies; the Southeast is warm with a long coastline.
  • The Midwest is flat farmland — often called the nation's breadbasket.
  • The Southwest is dry desert and canyons; the West has mountains, forests and the Pacific coast.
  • Landforms and climate decide what people grow, build and do for work in each region.

Sample worksheet questions

1. What decides the work people do in a region?
Answer: Its landforms and climate
2. Which region is warm with a long coastline?
Answer: The Southeast
3. How many regions is the United States usually divided into?
Answer: Five
4. Name the five regions.
Answer: Northeast, Southeast, Midwest, Southwest and West
5. Which region had the earliest colonies?
Answer: The Northeast
