What your child will learn — Environmental Science: Earth's Systems & Our Future

  • Earth's spheres interact: atmosphere (air), hydrosphere (water), geosphere (rock/soil), biosphere (life). A volcano touches all four!
  • The carbon cycle: plants absorb CO₂; animals and burning release it. Burning fossil fuels adds ancient carbon fast, strengthening the greenhouse effect and warming the climate.
  • The water cycle (evaporation → condensation → precipitation) and nutrient cycles recycle Earth's fixed resources endlessly.
  • Sustainability means meeting today's needs without wrecking tomorrow's: renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro), conservation, and biodiversity protection all buy the future time.

Sample worksheet questions

1. Define sustainability.
Answer: Meeting present needs without compromising future generations' ability to meet theirs
2. Name the three steps of the water cycle.
Answer: Evaporation, condensation, precipitation
3. Which sphere contains all living things?
Answer: The biosphere
4. Why does burning fossil fuels change the climate?
Answer: It rapidly releases ancient stored carbon as CO₂, intensifying the greenhouse effect
5. Name Earth's four spheres and what each contains.
Answer: Atmosphere (air), hydrosphere (water), geosphere (rock/soil), biosphere (living things)
